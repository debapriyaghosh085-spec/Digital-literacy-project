# Cybercrime Case Study – Phishing Attack

Phishing is a common type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data. This usually happens through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear to be from trusted sources like banks or popular services.

In a typical phishing attack, a student may receive an email claiming to be from a bank or an online platform, asking them to verify their account by clicking a link. The link redirects them to a fake website that looks similar to the original one. When the user enters their login credentials, the attacker captures the information and gains unauthorized access to the account.

Students and young users are often targeted because they may not be fully aware of such threats. The consequences of phishing can include financial loss, identity theft, and misuse of personal data.

This case highlights the importance of being cautious while handling online communications and verifying the authenticity of websites and emails before sharing any sensitive information.
